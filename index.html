Entering the State of Iḥrām

At the Miqat boundary
›

Before crossing the Miqat, purify yourself, wear the Ihram garments, and make the intention (Niyyah) for Umrah. Men wear two unstitched white cloths; women wear regular modest clothing covering all except face and hands.

لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ عُمْرَةً
Labbayka Allahumma 'umratan
"Here I am, O Allah, for Umrah." — The Niyyah
لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ لَبَّيْكَ، لَبَّيْكَ لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ لَبَّيْكَ
Labbayka Allahumma labbayk, labbayka la shareeka laka labbayk
The Talbiyah — recite continuously until Tawaf begins
💡 Perform Ghusl, trim nails, and remove unwanted hair before entering Ihram.
2

Entering Masjid al-Haram

The Grand Mosque
›

Enter with your right foot first. When you first see the Ka'bah, stop and make sincere dua — this is a moment of acceptance.

اللَّهُمَّ افْتَحْ لِي أَبْوَابَ رَحْمَتِكَ
Allahumma iftah li abwaba rahmatik
"O Allah, open for me the doors of Your mercy."
🕌 First sight of the Ka'bah is a moment of accepted dua — make your most sincere request.
3

Tawaf — 7 Circuits Around the Ka'bah

Counter-clockwise from the Black Stone
›

Begin at the Black Stone. Complete 7 counter-clockwise circuits. Men: expose right shoulder (Idtibaa), walk briskly in first 3 rounds (Raml). Kiss or point to the Black Stone saying "Allahu Akbar" each pass.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ
Bismillah, Allahu Akbar
Said at the Black Stone each circuit
رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ
Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanatan wa fil-akhirati hasanatan wa qina adhab an-nar
Between Yemeni Corner and Black Stone
⚡ Use our Tawaf Mode in the map section to follow each circuit with specific duas.
4

Prayer at Maqam Ibrahim

2 Rakat behind the Station of Ibrahim
›

Pray 2 Rakat behind Maqam Ibrahim — recite Surah Al-Kafirun in the first and Al-Ikhlas in the second. Then drink Zamzam water.

وَاتَّخِذُوا مِن مَّقَامِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ مُصَلًّى
Wattakhidhu min maqami Ibrahima musalla
"And take the station of Ibrahim as a place of prayer." (2:125)
💧 After the 2 Rakat, go to Zamzam well. Drink facing the Ka'bah making dua.
5

Sa'i — Between Safa & Marwa

7 walks, starting at Safa
›

Begin at Safa, walk to Marwa — one lap. Complete 7 laps, ending at Marwa. Men jog between the green lights. Commemorates Hajar's search for water.

إِنَّ الصَّفَا وَالْمَرْوَةَ مِن شَعَائِرِ اللَّهِ
Innas-Safa wal-Marwata min sha'a'irillah
Recited ascending Safa (Al-Baqarah 2:158)
🏃 The Sa'i corridor is air-conditioned and wheelchair accessible on the ground floor.
6

Halq or Taqsir — Cutting Hair

Men shave/shorten; women trim slightly
›

Men shave the head (Halq — more virtuous) or shorten all around (Taqsir). Women cut a fingertip length. This releases you from Ihram.

✂️ After this step all Ihram restrictions are lifted. You may wear regular clothes and use perfume.
7

🎉 Umrah Complete!

Make dua and give thanks
›

Alhamdulillah — your Umrah is complete! Make sincere dua for yourself, family, and the Ummah. Perform optional Tawaf al-Wida before leaving Makkah.

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي ب

📖 History & Significance

The sacred stories behind the holy sites

🕋
~2000 BCE · Foundation

The Ka'bah — House of Allah

Built by Prophet Ibrahim ﷺ and his son Ismail ﷺ as the first house of worship. As they laid the stones, they prayed: "Our Lord, accept this from us. You are the All-Hearing, the All-Knowing." (2:127). The Ka'bah is the qiblah — the direction Muslims face in all prayers worldwide.

⬛
Pre-Islamic · Sacred Stone

Hajar al-Aswad — The Black Stone

The Black Stone descended from Paradise white as milk but turned black from the sins of mankind. The Prophet ﷺ said: "The Black Stone descended from Paradise." It marks the start and end of each Tawaf circuit and is kissed or pointed to as an act of reverence.

👣
~2000 BCE · Miracle

Maqam Ibrahim — Station of Ibrahim

When Ibrahim ﷺ stood on a stone to build the Ka'bah, Allah softened it — leaving his footprints preserved within. The Quran commands: "Take the station of Ibrahim as a place of prayer." (2:125).

💧
~2000 BCE · Water of Life

Zamzam Well

When Ibrahim ﷺ left Hajar and infant Ismail in the barren valley, Hajar ran desperately between Safa and Marwa searching for water. Allah answered miraculously — the Zamzam spring gushed forth and has never dried in over 4,000 years.

🏃
~2000 BCE · Act of Faith

Safa & Marwa — The Sa'i Walk

Commemorates Hajar's seven runs between the two hills in search of water for infant Ismail. Her unshakeable trust in Allah is honored by every pilgrim who walks this path today.

🕌
630 CE — Present

Masjid al-Haram — The Grand Mosque

The Prophet ﷺ entered Makkah and purified the Ka'bah of idols in 630 CE. Today the mosque covers over 356,800 m² accommodating millions during Hajj. One prayer here equals 100,000 prayers elsewhere.

🌙
610 CE · First Revelation

Cave of Hira

On Jabal al-Nour, Angel Jibreel descended with the first revelation: "Read in the name of your Lord who created." (96:1). This moment marked the beginning of the Quran and changed the world forever.

رَبَّنَا تَقَبَّلْ مِنَّا إِنَّكَ أَنتَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
Our Lord, accept from us. Indeed, You are the Hearing, the Knowing.

👘 Complete Reference

The Ihram Guide

Everything you need to know — from preparing at home to entering the sacred state with confidence and correct intention.

🕊️

What is Ihram?

الإِحْرَام
Al-Iḥrām — from the Arabic root ḥ-r-m (حرم), meaning "forbidden" or "sacred"
Ihram is the sacred state that every Muslim must enter before performing Hajj or Umrah. It consists of two things: wearing the Ihram garments and making the intention (Niyyah). Once in Ihram, certain actions that are normally permitted become temporarily prohibited.

Ihram has two dimensions:

1. Physical: The distinctive garments worn — for men, two pieces of unstitched white cloth. For women, any modest, loose clothing that covers the body (except face and hands).

2. Spiritual: A state of heightened devotion, detachment from worldly matters, and complete focus on worship of Allah.

🌟
Why white? The Prophet ﷺ said: "Wear white clothes, for they are the best of your clothes, and shroud your dead in them." (Abu Dawud 4061). The simplicity reminds every pilgrim of equality before Allah and the Day of Resurrection.
🕐

When to Enter Ihram

For Umrah

Ihram for Umrah can be entered at any time of year — there are no restricted months for Umrah. You must enter Ihram before crossing the Miqat boundary on your way to Makkah.

Arriving by Air

If you fly directly to Jeddah or Madinah, you must put on Ihram and make your intention before the aircraft crosses the Miqat boundary. Airlines typically announce this. It is safer to wear Ihram before boarding your flight.

⚠️
Crossing Miqat without Ihram is a serious violation. If you pass the Miqat boundary without entering Ihram, you must either return to the Miqat to enter Ihram there, or slaughter a sacrificial animal (dam) as an expiation. Do not risk this.
📋
Tip for air travellers: Wear your Ihram garments at home or at the airport before departure. It is easier and safer than changing on the aircraft, and you can make the Niyyah when the Miqat is announced.
📍

Miqat Locations

The Miqat (مِيقَات) is the designated boundary point beyond which no person intending Hajj or Umrah may pass without being in a state of Ihram. There are five major Mawaqit (plural), each for pilgrims coming from a different direction.

Tap a numbered point on the map to highlight that Miqat below

① Dhul Hulayfah (Abyar Ali)
📍 For pilgrims from Madinah and those passing through it
~450 km north of Makkah — the farthest Miqat from Makkah. Also called Abyar Ali.
ذُو الْحُلَيْفَة
② Al-Juhfah (Rabigh)
📍 For pilgrims from Syria, Morocco, Egypt, and the West
Near Rabigh on the Red Sea coast, ~204 km northwest of Makkah.
الجُحْفَة
③ Qarn al-Manazil (al-Sayl)
📍 For pilgrims from Najd, UAE, and the East
~94 km east of Makkah. Also used by those arriving from Riyadh by road.
قَرْنُ الْمَنَازِل
④ Yalamlam
📍 For pilgrims from Yemen, India, Pakistan, and the South
~120 km south of Makkah. Used by most pilgrims from Southeast Asia.
يَلَمْلَم
⑤ Dhat Irq
📍 For pilgrims from Iraq and the Northeast
~94 km northeast of Makkah. The northernmost eastern Miqat.
ذَاتُ عِرْق
✈️
Arriving by air? Check your flight path. Pilgrims flying from Pakistan typically cross Yalamlam. Those from Turkey/Europe cross Al-Juhfah. Those from the Gulf may cross Qarn al-Manazil. When in doubt, enter Ihram before boarding.
🏙️
Already in Makkah? People who live in Makkah or within the Miqat boundaries enter Ihram from their home or the nearest point outside the Haram. For Umrah specifically, they typically go to al-Tan'im or al-Ji'ranah.
📋

How to Enter Ihram — Step by Step

1

Remove Unwanted Hair & Clip Nails

Trim or remove underarm hair, pubic hair, and clip fingernails and toenails before entering Ihram, since these become prohibited once in the state of Ihram.

2

Perform Ghusl (Full Body Wash)

Perform a complete ritual bath (ghusl) with the intention of purification for Ihram. This is a confirmed Sunnah. If ghusl is not possible, performing wudu (ablution) is sufficient.

3

Apply Perfume to Your Body (Men Only)

Men may apply perfume to the body only — not to the Ihram cloth. Once the Ihram is worn, perfume becomes prohibited. The Prophet ﷺ used to apply perfume before entering Ihram.

⚠️
Women: should not apply perfume or any scented products before or during Ihram.
4

Wear the Ihram Garments

Men: Wear two pieces of plain white unstitched cloth — an Izar (lower wrap, like a sarong) and a Rida (upper wrap draped over the left shoulder). No underwear, no stitched clothing.

Women: Wear any loose, modest clothing that covers the entire body except the face and hands. There is no specific colour required. No niqab (face cover) and no gloves while in Ihram.

5

Pray 2 Rakat (Sunnat al-Ihram)

Pray 2 rakat with the intention of Sunnat al-Ihram — recite Surah Al-Kafirun in the first and Surah Al-Ikhlas in the second. This prayer should be performed before making the Niyyah if you are at the Miqat and prayer time is not prohibited (makruh).

6

Make the Niyyah (Intention)

Face the Qiblah and pronounce your intention for Umrah at the Miqat. The intention is what officially enters you into the state of Ihram — the garments alone without the intention do not constitute Ihram.

لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ عُمْرَةً
Labbayka Allāhumma ʿumratan
"Here I am, O Allah, for Umrah."
7

Begin Reciting the Talbiyah

As soon as the Niyyah is made, begin reciting the Talbiyah. Men: raise your voice. Women: recite quietly. Continue the Talbiyah until you begin Tawaf around the Ka'bah.

لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ لَبَّيْكَ
Labbayka Allāhumma labbayk…
Begin the Talbiyah — repeat continuously
🤲

Intention (Niyyah)

The Niyyah is the sincere intention in the heart to begin Umrah for the sake of Allah. It is what makes the Ihram valid. Simply wearing the garments without the intention is not Ihram.

The intention can be made verbally (recommended) or purely in the heart. It must be made at or before the Miqat boundary.

لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ عُمْرَةً
Labbayka Allāhumma ʿumratan
"Here I am, O Allah, for Umrah."
Sahih Bukhari 1559 — Standard Niyyah for Umrah

What if you fear illness during Umrah?

You may add a conditional clause: "If something prevents me, then my Ihram ends where I am prevented." This is called Ishtiraat and protects you from having to make expiation if you become ill or unable to complete Umrah.

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أُرِيدُ الْعُمْرَةَ فَيَسِّرْهَا لِي وَتَقَبَّلْهَا مِنِّي
Allāhumma innī urīdu al-ʿumrata fa-yassirha lī wa taqabbalha minnī
"O Allah, I intend Umrah, so make it easy for me and accept it from me."
📯

The Talbiyah

لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ لَبَّيْكَ، لَبَّيْكَ لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ لَبَّيْكَ، إِنَّ الْحَمْدَ وَالنِّعْمَةَ لَكَ وَالْمُلْكَ، لَا شَرِيكَ لَكَ
Labbayka Allāhumma labbayk. Labbayka lā sharīka laka labbayk. Inna al-ḥamda wan-niʿmata laka wal-mulk. Lā sharīka lak.
"Here I am, O Allah, here I am. Here I am, You have no partner, here I am. Surely all praise, grace, and sovereignty belong to You. You have no partner."

Rules of the Talbiyah:

✦ Men should recite it loudly — this is a confirmed Sunnah.

✦ Women should recite it quietly, audible only to themselves.

✦ Recite it repeatedly — after prayers, when ascending or descending, when meeting other pilgrims, at dawn, and at all times you remember.

✦ Continue until you begin Tawaf. Once you start circling the Ka'bah, the Talbiyah stops.

💡
The Talbiyah is not just recitation — it is the pilgrim's response to Allah's call to visit His House. Reflect on its meaning as you say it: you are answering Allah's invitation.
⭐

Sunnahs of Ihram

These are recommended acts — following them earns extra reward, but Ihram is still valid without them:

★

Perform Ghusl before Ihram

Even menstruating women and those in a state of post-natal bleeding should perform ghusl for Ihram, though they cannot pray the 2 rakat.

★

Apply perfume to the body (men)

The Prophet ﷺ applied perfume to his body before entering Ihram. The scent remaining on the body during Tawaf is permissible even if visible.

★

Wear new or clean white cloth

The Prophet ﷺ recommended wearing new or freshly laundered white Ihram garments.

★

Pray 2 rakat before Niyyah

If it is not a prohibited time for prayer (e.g. at sunrise/sunset), pray 2 rakat before making your intention.

★

Recite Talbiyah after each prayer

Increase the Talbiyah especially after every prayer and when ascending or descending slopes.

🚫

What is Prohibited in Ihram

⚠️
Violating these prohibitions may require expiation (kaffarah). Some violations invalidate the Umrah itself. Ignorance does not always exempt a person — learn these before entering Ihram.

🚫 Prohibited for both Men and Women

✂️ Cutting or removing hair from any part of the body
💅 Clipping or cutting fingernails or toenails
🌸 Applying perfume or scented products to body or clothing
💍 Contracting marriage or proposing (for oneself)
🦌 Hunting land animals or helping anyone to hunt
🌿 Cutting or uprooting plants within the Haram boundary
❤️ Sexual intercourse or any sexual activity
💬 Obscene language, arguing, or immoral behaviour

🚫 Prohibited for Men Only

👕 Wearing stitched clothing of any kind
🧢 Covering the head with any garment
👞 Wearing shoes that cover the ankles
🧦 Wearing socks or any foot covering

🚫 Prohibited for Women Only

😷 Wearing a niqab (face veil) that touches the face
🧤 Wearing gloves on the hands
Consequences of Violations

Kaffarah (Expiation) for Minor Violations:

For violations such as cutting hair, clipping nails, or applying perfume — the expiation is one of the following three: fasting 3 days, OR feeding 6 poor people, OR slaughtering a sheep. (Quran 2:196)

For sexual intercourse before completing Umrah: the Umrah is invalidated. You must complete it, then make up a full Umrah on another occasion, and slaughter a sacrificial animal.

✅

What is Allowed in Ihram

Many normal activities remain fully permitted while in Ihram. Here are common questions answered:

🚿 Showering and washing the body with unscented soap
🏖️ Washing hair (gently, without deliberate pulling)
👓 Wearing glasses, watches, or hearing aids
💊 Using unscented medication or medical devices
☂️ Using an umbrella for shade from the sun
💰 Wearing a money belt or carrying a bag
🍎 Eating, drinking, and sleeping normally
💍 Wearing a ring already on the finger
🩺 Receiving medical treatment
🐛 Killing fleas, mosquitoes, and harmful insects
🛡️ Killing snakes, scorpions, dogs in self-defence
🩴 Wearing sandals (for men — must not cover the ankle)
💡
Hair falling naturally — hair that falls out on its own while washing or combing is not a violation, as long as you are not deliberately removing it.
👒
Head shade vs head cover (Men): Sitting under an umbrella or car roof is permitted. Placing any cloth, hat, or garment directly on the head is prohibited. Holding an umbrella above the head is fine.
❌

Common Mistakes to Avoid

✈️

Passing the Miqat without Ihram

The most common and serious mistake. Many first-time pilgrims are not aware they are crossing the Miqat boundary mid-flight and fail to enter Ihram in time.

✓ Fix: Wear Ihram before boarding — it is always safer
🧴

Using scented soap, shampoo, or lotion

Many pilgrims do not check whether their soap, shampoo, toothpaste, or sunscreen is scented. Scented products trigger expiation (kaffarah).

✓ Fix: Pack unscented versions of all toiletries before travel
🧢

Men covering their head

Some men habitually wear a hat or towel over their head, even briefly, not realising this is prohibited in Ihram.

✓ Fix: Use an umbrella for shade instead
💬

Arguing or losing temper

The Quran explicitly forbids obscenity, wickedness, and argumentation during Ihram. (2:197) Crowds can be stressful, but patience is essential.

✓ Fix: Recite Talbiyah and dhikr to stay focused and calm
💍

Removing rings and jewellery (women)

Some women mistakenly believe they must remove all jewellery. Wearing jewellery is allowed in Ihram for women as long as it is not done as an adornment in front of non-mahram men.

✓ Fix: Jewellery worn before Ihram can remain on
🌿

Clipping nails or removing hair for hygiene

Even if a nail breaks or hair is uncomfortable, cutting them while in Ihram requires expiation unless done out of genuine medical necessity.

✓ Fix: Clip all nails and trim hair thoroughly before entering Ihram
❓

Frequently Asked Questions

Can women wear any colour in Ihram?›
Yes. There is no specific colour requirement for women's Ihram. Most scholars say any modest, loose, covering garment is acceptable. White is popular but not mandatory for women. Black abayas are widely used and entirely valid.
Can I shower or bathe while in Ihram?›
Yes — showering is permitted and even recommended for hygiene. Use unscented soap and shampoo. Wash gently and do not deliberately pull out hair. If hair falls naturally during washing, there is no violation.
What if my Ihram cloth becomes dirty or torn?›
You may wash your Ihram cloth with unscented soap. You may also change to another piece of Ihram cloth — the state of Ihram remains valid as long as the Niyyah remains and you have not performed Halq/Taqsir. Changing the physical cloth does not break Ihram.
Can I use an inhaler or medication while in Ihram?›
Yes. Medical necessity is accommodated. Unscented inhalers, medications, IV drips, and medical devices are all permitted. If a scented medication is medically required, use it and make expiation if needed — your health takes priority.
What if I am menstruating when I reach the Miqat?›
A menstruating woman still enters Ihram at the Miqat — she performs ghusl, wears Ihram, makes the Niyyah, and recites Talbiyah. She should not perform Tawaf until her period ends and she has purified herself. She can perform all other rites of Umrah before and after Tawaf once purified.
Can a woman's face veil (niqab) be worn in Ihram?›
A niqab (face veil attached to or touching the face) is prohibited for women in Ihram. However, women must cover their face if non-mahram men are close — this is done by pulling the headscarf or cloth forward to cover the face without it being attached or sewn to the garment. The prohibition is specifically against a face veil that is tailored/shaped for the face.
Is it permissible to sleep on the airplane in Ihram?›
Yes, sleeping in Ihram is fully permitted. The only concern is that your Ihram cloth does not fall off or shift to cover the head during sleep (for men). Try to secure it before sleeping, and check upon waking.
What ends the state of Ihram?›
For Umrah, the state of Ihram ends after you complete all rites — Tawaf, prayer at Maqam Ibrahim, Sa'i — and then perform Halq (shave) or Taqsir (hair trim). It is the act of Halq/Taqsir that officially releases you from Ihram, not simply removing the garment.
Can children be in Ihram?›
Yes. A child who has reached the age of understanding may enter Ihram and perform Umrah. The guardian (wali) may make the Niyyah on behalf of a young child who cannot understand. The child's Umrah is valid and earns reward, though it does not substitute for the obligatory adult Umrah.
What if I forget to make the Niyyah at the Miqat?›
If you crossed the Miqat without making Niyyah (and without wearing Ihram), you must go back to the Miqat to enter Ihram — if this is feasible. If you cannot return, you enter Ihram from where you are and must slaughter an animal (dam) as expiation. Consult a knowledgeable scholar in your specific situation.
